_____ Big Picture Items (38%)

_____ Choice of Topic (6%)

6:  The topic is appropriate to the course focused narrowly enough for the scope of this assignment.

4   The topic is appropriate to the course and focused, but lacks direction.

2:  The topic is appropriate to the course, but too broad or too narrow for the scope of this assignment.

0:  The topic is appropriate to the course and/or not clearly defined.

_____ Organization (8%)

8: Writer demonstrates logical sequencing of ideas with a specific point for each paragraph.

6: Paragraph/section development present but not perfected.

4: Logical organization; organization of ideas not fully developed.

2: Limited evidence of structure or organization.

0: No evidence of structure or organization.

_____ Integration of Knowledge (8%)

8:  The paper demonstrates that the author fully understands the references being reviewed and has applied concepts learned in the course. Concepts are integrated into the writer’s own insights. The writer provides concluding remarks that show analysis and synthesis of ideas, with conclusions supported in both the body of the paper and cited sources. 

6:  The paper demonstrates that the author, for the most part, understands the references being reviewed and has applied concepts learned in the course. Some of the conclusions, however, are not supported in the body of the paper and cited sources. 

4:  The paper demonstrates that the author, to a certain extent, understands the references being reviewed and has applied concepts learned in the course.

2:  The paper does not demonstrate that the author has fully understood the references being reviewed or that the author has not applied concepts learned in the course.

0:  The paper does not demonstrate that the author has fully understood the references being reviewed and that the author has not applied concepts learned in the course.

_____ Depth of Discussion (8%)

8:  In-depth discussion & elaboration in all sections of the paper.

6:  In-depth discussion & elaboration in most sections of the paper.

4:  In-depth discussion & elaboration in some sections of the paper.

2:  The writer has omitted pertinent content or content runs-on excessively. Quotations from others outweigh the writer’s own ideas excessively.

0:  Cursory discussion in all the sections of the paper or brief discussion in only a few sections.

_____ Cohesiveness (8%)

8:  Ties together information from all sources. Paper flows from one issue to the next without the need for headings. Author's writing demonstrates an understanding of the relationship among material obtained from all sources.

6:  For the most part, ties together information from all sources. Paper flows with only some disjointedness. Author's writing demonstrates an understanding of the relationship among material obtained from all sources.

4:  Sometimes ties together information from all sources. Paper does not flow - disjointedness is apparent. Author's writing does not demonstrate an understanding of all the relationship among material obtained from all sources.

2:  Does not tie together information. Paper does not flow and appears to be created from disparate issues. Writing does not demonstrate understanding of multiple relationships among material obtained from all sources.

0:  Absent, no cohesiveness

_____ Specific Elements of Paper (30%)

_____ Thesis Statement (6%)

6:  Clearly and concisely states the paper’s purpose in a single sentence, which is engaging, and thought provoking.  The thesis statement provides direction for the paper and with an interpretation based on evidence presented in the sources.

4:  Clearly states the paper’s purpose in a single sentence.

2:  States the paper’s purpose in a single sentence.

1:  Incomplete and/or unfocused.

0:  Absent, no evidence

_____ Introduction (6%)

6:  The introduction establishes the importance of the research topic, previews the structure of the paper, and states the main topic in an engaging way.

4:  The introduction addresses the importance of the research topic, but leaves room for improvement, and states the main topic and previews the structure of the paper. 

2:  The introduction does not establish the importance of the research topic, or states the main topic but does not adequately preview the structure of the paper.

1:  There is no clear introduction or main topic and the structure of the paper is missing.

0:  Absent, no evidence

_____ Body (6%)

6: Each paragraph has thoughtful supporting detail sentences that develop the main idea.

4: Each paragraph has sufficient supporting detail sentences that develop the main idea.

2: Some paragraphs lacks supporting detail sentences.

1: Multiple paragraph fails to develop the main idea.

0: Not applicable

_____ Conclusion (6%)

6. The conclusion is engaging and restates the thesis.

4.  The conclusion restates the thesis.

2.  The conclusion does not adequately restate the thesis.

1.  Incomplete and/or unfocused.

0.  Absent

_____ Quality of References (6)

6: More than 3 current and appropriate sources, of which at least 1 is a peer-review journal article. Sources include both general background sources and specialized sources. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

4: At least 3 current and appropriate sources, of which at least 1 is a peer-review journal article, scientific manuscript submitted for publication in a peer-reviewed journal or conference proceedings or scholarly book. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

2: At least 2 appropriate sources. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

1: At least 1 appropriate source. All web sites utilized are credible. 0: Not all web sites utilized are credible, and/or some sources are not appropriate.

_____ Writing Mechanics & Style (32%)

_____ Mechanics (8%)

8:  No errors in punctuation, capitalization and spelling.  Professional style throughout

6:  Almost no errors in punctuation, capitalization, spelling or professionalism.

4:  Several errors in punctuation, capitalization,  spelling and/or professionalism.

2:  Numerous and/or distracting errors in punctuation, capitalization, spelling, and/or professionalism.

0:  Not applicable

_____ Usage (8%)

8:  No errors in sentence structure or word usage.

6:  Almost no errors in sentence structure or word usage.

4:  Several errors in sentence structure and/or word usage.

2:  Numerous and/or particularly distracting errors in sentence structure and/or word usage.

0:  Not applicable

_____ Citation Style (8%)

8:  All cited works are done in the correct format with no errors.

6:  Some cited works are done in the correct format.

4:  Inconsistencies evident.

2:  Few cited works are done in the correct format.

0:  Absent/Not applicable

_____ Reference Style (8%)

8:  Done in the correct format with no errors.

6:  Done in the correct format with few errors. 

4:  Done in the correct format with some errors.

2:  Done in the correct format with several errors.

0:  Absent

Rubric for Draft or Detailed Outline of Scientific Paper

_____ Organization (40%)

40: Writer demonstrates logical sequencing of ideas with a specific point for each paragraph.

30: Paragraph/section development present but not perfected.

20: Logical organization; organization of ideas not fully developed.

10: Limited evidence of structure or organization.

0:  No evidence of structure or organization.

_____ Level of Detail (20%)

20: A specific purpose for each paragraph and at least two appropriate supporting details.

10: Room for improvement in purpose or level of supporting details of some paragrams.

0:  Room for improvement in purpose and level of supporting details of multiple paragrams

_____ Quality of References (40%)

40: More than 3 current and appropriate sources, of which at least 1 is a peer-review journal article. Sources include both general background sources and specialized sources. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

30: At least 3 current and appropriate sources, of which at least 1 is a peer-review journal article, scientific manuscript submitted for publication in a peer-reviewed journal or conference proceedings or scholarly book. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

20: At least 2 appropriate sources. Special-interest sources and popular literature are acknowledged as such if they are cited. All web sites utilized are authoritative.

10: At least 1 appropriate source. All web sites utilized are credible.

0:  Not all web sites utilized are credible, and/or some sources are not appropriate.

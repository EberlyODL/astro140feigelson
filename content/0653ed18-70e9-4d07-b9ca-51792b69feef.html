The recommended topics for Paper 2 concern exoplanet discoveries, properties, and implications.  While you can rely on information designed for the public (news releases, Wikipedia), I hope that most of you will delve into the research literature, at least for part of your paper. You can find the research papers on the `arXiv e-print' button of the ADS Web site.   Building on your experience with Paper I, your essay should be well-organized with an introduction / motivation, substantive scientific content, and brief conclusion.  Length is 2-3 pages of text (single spaced) with added figures and references.  The `science writing' style should be enforced: after you draft the text, revise it to remove `I/We' opinions, weak sentence structures (`This is ...'), colloquialisms (`once in a while...'), exaggerations (`amazing'), and excessive quotes.  Emphasize clear presentation of scientific findings and interpretations.

TRAPPIST 1.  One of the most exciting exoplanetary systems, TRAPPIST 1 is a very nearby (12 parsecs), very low-mass star with roughly 10% the size and mass of our Sun.  It has seven (!) detected transiting Earth-sized planets;  three reside in the Habitable Zone.  Dozens research papers have been written about this system in the past two years, so your essay should concentrate on some particular aspect: models for its formation; the `resonance’ and ‘tidal locking’ of the planets; atmospheres of the planets; evidence for water on the planets; effects of stellar flares and X-rays on habitability.  Wikipedia has an excellent article that lays the groundwork, and the following references provide details on different aspects of the system.
